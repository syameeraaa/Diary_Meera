My Life’s Backbone

My Lovely Ayah🧔🌷

Here, I want to share a little about my father, Mohd Fauzi bin Ramli. My father was born on September 9,1970 in Muar,Johor, and he was 54 years old when he passed away. He got married later in life, at the age of 33. My dad was a hardworking man who always did his best to support our family. He never gave up and worked tirelessly to provide us with the best life he could. I am the youngest one in the family, and I am very lucky to have had a father who was loving and strong. Here is a picture of me with my dad when I was little. Losing my father, especially since I was so close to him, was not easy for me to accept at first. But over time, I have learned to accept his passing. Even though my heart still feels empty, I try to accept the reality that my dad is gone. I miss his jokes and the way he always knew how to make me smile. Thank you, dad, for everything you did for us, your love, and the lessons you taught me. I will always keep the beautiful memories of you in my heart and pray that Allah blesses your soul.


My Syurga🧕💐

My mother, Rusnani Binti Pandi, is a quiet and loving woman. At the age of 55, she has worked for almost 40 years to ensure the comfort and well-being of her children. Even though she doesn’t speak much, her love for me is always clear. When I was studying in Rembau, she would always check on me, making sure I had enough food, money, and didn’t miss any classes. Every word from her was a sign of how much she cared for me. Since my father passed away, I try to go home every two weeks to keep her company and make sure she doesn't feel lonely. I know nothing can replace the time spent with our parents. My mother also loves to cook. Every time I crave her cooking, even after a long day at work, she always makes the effort to fulfill my wish with love. My hope is to make my mother proud because I know my late father would be happy to see my success in the future. I want to repay her sacrifices and show her that all her hard work and love have shaped me into someone who strives to achieve great things. I dream of giving her a comfortable and peaceful life, just as she has always given me.


My Sister 💕

My sister, Nurelyna Syamimie, is 21 years old and currently pursuing a degree in Chemical Engineering at Universiti Teknologi Petronas (UTP) in Sri Iskandar, Perak. She’s not just brilliant she’s incredibly dedicated, having scored straight 11A's in her SPM, and her dream is to continue her studies all the way to a PhD. What makes her even more special is how caring and thoughtful she is, especially when it comes to family. She constantly reminds us about the importance of religion, always guiding us with wisdom. Even though she’s far away, she makes sure to stay connected by calling and messaging every day to check on us, making sure we’re okay. We didn’t always get along. In fact, there was a time when we fought a lot, and it would often make her cry. My rebellious nature made things difficult, but through it all, she showed remarkable patience. Over the years, we've learned to appreciate and understand each other better. Now, we share a bond that is built on trust, love, and the many experiences we’ve been through together. I look back on our childhood with a mixture of laughter and nostalgia. Although there were times we didn’t get along and even fought, those moments, in hindsight, are the ones that shaped our relationship. They’ve become some of the most unforgettable memories of our lives. Despite the ups and downs, I wouldn’t trade those times for anything because they’ve helped me grow closer to my sister, who has been my pillar in ways I never realized until now.
